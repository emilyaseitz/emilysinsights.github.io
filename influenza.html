PROJECT LIMITATIONS

THE BELOW ILLUSTRATES CHALLENGES IN THE DATA USED IN THE PROJECT PLAN.

DATA LIMITATIONS
THE INFLUENZA VISIT AND LAB TESTS DATA FROM THE CDC CAME FROM A SMALL SAMPLE OF CLINICS AND DOES NOT REPRESENT ALL POPULATIONS.
FLU SHOT DATA FROM THE CDC WAS COLLECTED BY UNIVERSITY OF CHICAGO AND VALIDATED BY PHYSICIANS FROM PARENTS GIVING PERMISSION AND IS NOT REPRESENTATIVE OF THE TOTAL POPULATION.
ALL DATASETS WERE COLLECTED BEFORE 2020 (PRE-PANDEMIC) AND DO NOT REFLECT THE MOST CURRENT TREND.
THERE COULD BE SHIFTS IN POPULATIONS SINCE THESE DATA SETS WERE COLLECTED DUE TO SO MANY PEOPLE MOVING WITH LARGE SOCIETAL CHANGES WITH FLEXIBLE REMOTE WORKING POST 2020.

SAMPLE VISUALIZATIONS

WHICH POPULATIONS ARE THE MOST VULNERABLE TO DEATH AS A RESULT OF FLU?

  • THOSE OVER 75 HAVE THE GREATEST RISK OF DEATH.
  • BOTH 85 AND OLDER AND 75-84 YEAR OLDS MAKE UP THE MAJORITY OF DEATHS IN THE >75 DEATH COUNTS FREQUENCIES.
  • 65-74 AND 55-64 YEAR OLDS MAKE UP THE NEXT LARGEST % OF DEATHS, BUT MAINLY FALL IN THE LOWER <50 DEATH COUNTS.
  • 45-54 YEAR OLDS MAKE UP A SMALL % OF THE <25 DEATH COUNTS.

    • ARE THE HIGHEST DEATHS CONCENTRATED BY POPULATION SIZE?

    THE HIGH POPUlOUS STATES IN BLUE WITH DARK GREY SQUARES ILLUSTRATE HIGHER MORTALITY RATES. THERE ARE OUTLIERS OF HIGHER MORTALITY RATES IN LESSER POPULATED AREAS AS WELL AS INDICATED IN MAP BY DARK GREY SQUARES IN YELLOW SHADED LOWER POPULOUS STATES.
    THERE IS A STRONG CORRELATION THAT PROVES DEATHS ARE RELATED TO POPULATION SIZE.

    WHEN ARE THE PEAK MONTHS OF FLU SEASON?

    DATA SHOWS WHILE INFLUENZA INFECTIONS OCCUR YEAR LONG; DEATHS SPIKE IN WINTER MONTHS OF DECEMBER GOING THRU MARCH.

    INFLUENZA PLAN RECOMENDATIONS

    RECOMENDATIONS ARE BASED ON THE INSIGHTS FROM POPULATION AND INFLUENZA DEATHS FROM THE CDC DATASETS WHICH GUIDE PLANNING PREPARATION BASED ON THE TIMING, AGE VULNERABILITIES IN REGIONAL POPULATIONS.

    CONCLUSION: THE BELOW ARE IN SEASON RECOMENDATIONS TO REACT TO INITAL PLANS AND ANY CHANGES THAT EMERGE IN THIS YEAR'S FLU SEASON.

    IN SEASON PLANNING

    ACTION STEPS
    EXECUTE PLAN FOR STAFF NEED BASED ON TIMING AND LOCATIONS OF HIGHEST RISK POPULATION BY GEOGRAPHY BASED ON INSIGHTS FROM CDC DATA.
    SEND ADDITIONAL STAFF PRE-PEAK MONTHS TO SUPPORT ADMINISTERING PREVENTATIVE VACCINES IN HIGH RISK POPULOUS POPULATIONS AND IN-SEASON HAVE ADDITIONAL SUPPORT IN PLACE TO CARE FOR SICK PATIENTS.
    UPDATE KEY STAKEHOLDERS MONTHLY DURING PEAK SEASON VIA EMAIL ON EXECUTION OF STAFFING PLAN AND MONITOR PLANNED COVERAGE TO CURRENT STATISTICS ON OUTBREAKS AND ADJUST BASED ON NEW TRENDS THAT EMERGE.
    RESPOND TO ANY URGENT ISSUES THAT EMERGE WITHIN 72 HOURS AND ARRANGE CALL TO DISCUSS WITH STAKEHOLDERS.
    MONITOR THAT SUCCESS OF CARE TO ILLNESS RATIO IS HITTING IDEAL OF 91-110% BY LOCATION AND ADDRESS ANY OUTLIERS.
    RECONVENE END OF FLU SEASON IN MAY TO DETERMINE SUCCESS TO PLAN AS WELL TO IDENTIFY ANY NEW EMERGING TRENDS TO ADDRESS NEXT YEAR.
